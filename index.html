The rise of the UX field has been a story of meeting obstacles with persistence, creativity, and conviction. After decades of hustle, we’re starting to hit our stride. So what better place to bring together our UX community than Cincinnati, a city that’s met enormous challenges with midwestern grit and an explosive creativity all our own. We’ve been building momentum for a while now, with an emerging urban revitalization, a growing startup scene, and a network of flourishing creative communities. Join us in the Queen City for a UX conference that takes a minute to celebrate what we’ve accomplished in our industry, and to look forward to the work ahead.

Why Us?

Our group is a mix of UX pros from various agencies and in-house teams. Our proposed chair and co-chair are the organizers of Cincinnati User Enjoyment, a monthly meetup group with over 500 members. We’re not professional event planners. None of us have attempted anything like this before. All we know is that when we've attended MWUX in the past, we came back feeling inspired, connected, and fired up. We want to bring that same great experience to our city, and we want to show off Cincinnati and it’s budding UX community to the region.

Design in Cincinnati

There’s a huge design scene in Cincinnati. Ten different Fortune 500 companies are headquartered here, and a thriving community of traditional and digital design agencies support them. Most notably, we’re home to Procter & Gamble, the consumer products giant behind brands like Tide, Pampers and Bounty, and the world’s largest advertiser by annual spend.

On the tech side, Entrepreneur magazine called us an “unexpected hub for tech startups.” The Cincinnati-based Brandery earned a spot on Tech Cocktail’s list of Top 15 startup accelerators in the country. More than half of the Brandery’s graduates keep their companies in Cincinnati, and these start-ups are fueling growth in the local UX talent pool. The Cincinnati User Enjoyment UX Meetup, for example, has more than 300 members.

Creative Landmarks

Just a few highlights of Cincinnati’s creative and design scene:

DAAP: The University of Cincinnati College of Design, Art, Architecture and Planning

This local design school is world famous and consistently finds itself at the top of many best of lists. It makes regular appearances on Business Week’s list of top 30 design schools on the planet. And has received similar recognition from I.D. (International Design) magazine, U.S. News & World report and GDUSA magazine.

HOW and PRINT magazines, conferences and design contests

We’re home to two of the most popular design magazines in the country and the talented teams behind them. HOW runs a large interactive design competition each year and creates a wide range of online and in-person events for interactive designers. There’s the potential for creative collaboration or sponsorship for Midwest UX.

Cincinnati Represents

We have thriving local chapters of many notable national groups:

  • AIGA Cincinnati
  • Cincinnati Girl Develop It (1,000+ strong!)
  • Creative Mornings Cincinnati

Thriving Visual Arts Scene

We’re home to the Cincinnati Art Museum, Contemporary Arts Center, Taft Museum of Art and many other smaller galleries and museums. Local non-profit ArtWorks has created more than 90 public murals around the city, and Paste Magazine named us one of 11 Lesser-Known Cities for Street art. There are also regular art walks and open studios in neighborhoods throughout the city.

The Venue

The Aronoff Center

We envision Midwest UX Cincinnati taking place in the Aronoff Center -- a stunning performing arts center downtown with a mix of theaters, art galleries and meeting rooms. It’s an inspiring space with plenty of room for all-conference talks, breakout sessions and mingling in-between.

Main Talks

Jarson-Kaplan Theater: The Jarson-Kaplan Theater seats 437 and is a perfect setting for Midwest UX talks for all attendees. The theater is surprisingly intimate with full production capabilities, great acoustics, an attractive design, and excellent sightlines.

Break Outs

Fifth Third Bank Theater: The Fifth Third Bank Theater is a studio space that seats up to 150 in a theater setting, and offers full lighting and sound.

Green Room: This elegant reception space can accommodate up to 140 people for meetings and presentations.

Weston Art Gallery: This two-floor, 3,5000 square-foot art gallery provides a creative backdrop for one--or possibly even two--breakout sessions. The exact capacity varies depending on the size of the current art installation.

Contemporary Arts Center: Just across the street, this stunning modern building--designed by noted architect Zaha Hadid--will provide overflow space if we need additional breakouts. Or just want a fresh, art-filled space for a cocktail party or meal.

Lunch + Mingling

Aronoff Center Lobbies: A soaring wall of glass reveals three lobby levels, and each level can provide a stunning site for social events or meetings. All three floors together can seat 400 people for a meal.

Getting Around

It’s easy to explore downtown Cincinnati and the nearby Over the Rhine (OTR) neighborhood; the places where all the Midwest UX action will happen. We hope to provide free passes and/or discounts for some transportation options in attendee SWAG bags.

Cincinnati Streetcar

Midwest UX attendees will be among the first passengers on Cincinnati’s brand new street car! It’s set to open in mid September 2016. The route runs between downtown and historic OTR with stops every two blocks.

Cincinnati Red Bike

There are more than two dozen Cincinnati Red Bike stations in the city center. Attendees can easily rent a bike and roll from the hotel to the conference to dinner. Or take a leisurely ride along the banks of the Ohio River.

Cincinnati Metro

Downtown is the hub for Cincinnati’s bus system. It’s easy to go from one side of downtown to the other or explore the whole city. The free Bus Detective app gives real-time arrival data, so conference-goers can find out when the bus is coming.

Uber Cincinnati

An affordable private driver is a just a few clicks away with the abundance of Uber drivers serving Cincinnati. It only takes a few minutes to secure a ride in the downtown area.

User Experience is a story of dogged perseverance where unyielding creativity, innovation and conviction has guided us to where we are today. After decades of hustle, we have hit our stride and have no intentions of slowing down. There is no better place to bring together the UX community than Cincinnati. A city that has met seemingly insurmountable challenges with unique midwestern grit and ingenuity. The Queen City has awakened a renaissance in itself. With emerging urban revitalization, a flourishing startup scene, and a thriving network of creative communities, there is no place that sings quite like Cincinnati.

Join us in the Queen City for a UX conference that celebrates the hustle in our city and profession. Be a part of the momentum of Cincinnati and help us drive toward the future.

Why Us?

Our group is a mix of UX pros from various agencies and in-house teams. Our proposed chair and co-chair are the organizers of Cincinnati User Enjoyment, a monthly meetup group with over 500 members. We’re not professional event planners, and none of us have attempted anything like this before. All we know is that we’ve all attended MWUX in the past and come back feeling inspired, connected, and fired up. We want to bring that same awesomeness to our city, and to introduce our budding UX community to the region.

Design, UX and Tech in Cincinnati

Design

Cincinnati is home to the excellent University of Cincinnati College of Design, Art, Architecture and Planning, a school that regularly appears of “best of” lists in publications like Business Week, as well as two of the most popular design publications in the country, HOW and PRINT.

Our fine arts scene ranges from the formal galleries of the Cincinnati Art Museum, Contemporary Arts Center, Taft Museum of Art, to the more the 90 public murals created in collaboration with the local non-profit ArtWorks that have become the signatures of many neighborhoods.

Business

Cincinnati boasts everything from fortune 500 companies (ahem, 10 to be exact) to a thriving startup scene bolstered by accelerator programs like The Brandery. All this activity supports a number of digital design agencies, UX consultancies, and in-house teams.

Community

Residents of the Queen City always find a way to come together around their passions. Whether you’re looking to branch out into making your own cheese or writing your own app, chances are there is a group or a meetup filled with midwestern-nice enthusiasts who would love to show you the ropes.

We have local branches of national groups like:

  • AIGA Cincinnati
  • IXDA
  • Girl Develop It
  • Product Camp
  • Creative Mornings

We also have our own unique community groups. Cincinnati User Enjoyment is a local monthly meetup founded in 2012. It has grown to over 500 members, and is regularly attended by a mix of industry pros and those just getting into the field. The QCMerge Drinkup is a gathering of web and tech nerds that meets monthly to talk shop over beers, and currently has over 1,100 members.

The Venue

The Aronoff Center

The Arnoff has the right mix of beautiful modern architecture, downtown location, and a variety of gathering spaces and theaters to make it the perfect location for Midwest UX.

Main Talks

Jarson-Kaplan Theater: This theater space seats 437 in three tiers, offering everyone in the room a great view of the speakers.

Break Outs

Fifth Third Bank Theater: The Fifth Third Bank Theater is a studio space that seats up to 150 in a theater setting, and offers full lighting and sound.

Green Room: This space can accomodate 140 people and can be arranged for workshops and presentations alike.

Weston Art Gallery: This two-floor, 3,5000 square-foot art gallery provides a creative backdrop for one or two breakout sessions. The exact capacity varies depending on the size of the current art installation.

Contemporary Arts Center: Just across the street, this stunning modern building--designed by noted architect Zaha Hadid--will provide overflow space for workshops if we need it, but would also make s great location for a happy hour or lunch

Hotel Options

These hotels hit a variety of price points, and they’re all within easy walking distance to Midwest UX venues, bars, restaurants, museums, parks, the riverfront and more.

The Westin Cincinnati

Many of the rooms in this contemporary hotel overlook Fountain Square, a public event space in the heart of downtown. There are two restaurants located right in the hotel, including Ingredients for a fast salad or sandwich and McCormick & Schmick’s Seafood for a fancier meal. A lobby bar is the perfect spot for chatting with a new conference pal. Or it’s a short walk to dozens of other bars and restaurants.

Total rooms: 456

Event group discount rate: $189 a night

21C Museum Hotel

21c Museum Hotel Cincinnati was named the #1 Hotel in the Midwest, #2 Hotel in the United States and #29 in the world in the 2014 Conde Nast Traveler Readers’ Choice Awards. It’s a boutique hotel with a contemporary art gallery inside, as well as a bar and award-winning restaurant. 21C is located in the center of downtown, right next door to the Contemporary Arts Center and across the street from the Aronoff Center for the Arts.

Total rooms: 156

Event group discount rate: $149 a night

Residence Inn

Book boutique accommodations at Residence Inn Cincinnati Downtown, housed in the historic Phelps Building in the heart of the city. Total rooms: 134

  • Corporate Relations/Sponsorships Chair (immediately)
  • Design Chair (immediately)
  • Volunteer Coordinator Chair (no later than 9 months before event)
  • Marketing & Public Relations Chair (immediately)

    • Venue & service

  • Establish location, time & date for Midwest UX Cincinnati
  • Book venue: make deposits and sign contracts if necessary

Getting Around

It’s easy to explore downtown Cincinnati and the nearby Over the Rhine (OTR) neighborhood; the places where all the Midwest UX action will happen. We hope to provide free passes and/or discounts for some transportation options in attendee SWAG bags.

Cincinnati Streetcar

Midwest UX attendees will be among the first passengers on Cincinnati’s brand new street car! It’s set to open in mid September 2016. The route runs between downtown and historic OTR with stops every two blocks.

Cincinnati Red Bike

There are more than two dozen Cincinnati Red Bike stations in the city center. Attendees can easily rent a bike and roll from the hotel to the conference to dinner. Or take a leisurely ride along the banks of the Ohio River.

Cincinnati Metro

Downtown is the hub for Cincinnati’s bus system. It’s easy to go from one side of downtown to the other or explore the whole city. The free Bus Detective app gives real-time arrival data, so conference-goers can find out when the bus is coming.

Uber Cincinnati

An affordable private driver is a just a few clicks away with the abundance of Uber drivers serving Cincinnati. It only takes a few minutes to secure a ride in the downtown area.
